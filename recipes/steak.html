Steak

Description

Steak cooked like a chef - pan seared and basted with garlic thyme butter!

Ingredients

  • 1-2 boneless ribeye or scotch fillet, 2.5 cm / 1" thick, approx 300g/10 oz each
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • 75g / 5 tbsp unsalted butter, cut into 1.25 cm / 1/2" cubes
  • 6 sprigs fresh thyme or 3 sprigs rosemary
  • 5 garlic cloves, peeled and smashed to split

Steps

  1. Bring steaks to room temp 30 minutes prior
  2. Dry steaks: Pat dry with paper towels
  3. Heat skillet: Heat oil in a heavy based skillet over high heat until it is very hot - you should see smoke!
  4. Season: Sprinkle each side generously with salt and pepper, then immediately place in skillet
  5. Sear side 1: Cook for 2 - 2.5 minutes until you get a great crust, then turn
  6. Garlic butter: Leave for 1 minute then push steaks to one side and toss in butter, garlic, and thyme. Be careful - the thyme will sputter!
  7. Baste: As soon as the butter is melted, continuously spoon the butter over the steak until it's cooked to your liking - 2 minutes in total for the 2nd side for medium rare (52C/125F)
  8. Basting aso renders fat on the side of the steak - use tongs to sear the edges at the end if you want it browned more
  9. Rest: Transfer steak to a plate and cover loosely with foil, rest for 5 to 10 minutes
  10. Serve steak with a bit of the butter from the skillet drizzled on top
